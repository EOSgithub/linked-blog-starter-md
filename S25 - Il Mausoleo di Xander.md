## Xander Lore
This is the burial tomb of Xander Huorath, first leader of the Dragonlord Order and the greatest warrior in mortal history.

The structure is in tune with the rest of the Necropolis: it is a place meant to serve both as a mausoleum, preserving Xander’s memory, and a fortress, protecting the treasures and the body of the Dragonlords. However, while other tombs did this only through traps, the Tomb of Xander is a fortress in and of itself, structured like a labyrinth and guarded by the mighty minotaur [Graxis, the Butcher](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.google.com/url?q%3Dhttps://drive.google.com/file/d/1XhbxTzLbXj4T1S2JSnohHdndTjJgSJkR/view?usp%253Dsharing%26amp;sa%3DD%26amp;source%3Deditors%26amp;ust%3D1749456558933711%26amp;usg%3DAOvVaw29u6SzQsXHJQPJ6rgUrAwY&sa=D&source=docs&ust=1749456559098559&usg=AOvVaw3vnApUWSsSp3ZFjLDzYyi9), along with hoplite skeletons and other creatures and traps.

Graxis was part of one of the many minotaur tribes defeated by Rizon Phobas. His was, however, a special one: corrupted by Lutheria beyond their original curse. After they were conquered by Rizon, Damon cursed the survivors to live forever as his servants, controlled instead of eradicated; eventually, they would become guardians of the Necropolis, immortal minotaur skeletons even after their time had passed.

Graxis rebelled against this fate as much as he could. In rage, he killed his kindred who gave up and submitted; and for decades, he attempted to provoke Damon into battle or giving him a worse fate, as long as not in servitude. Even now, while he cannot truly abandon his duty, the minotaur often leaves the Necropolis to hunt around Telamok.

Additionally, the minotaur has taken part of Xander’s equipment, which rested on pedestals in his sarcophagus, specifically the Axe of Xander and the Golden Shield of Volkan, and uses them while inside the maze. Xander’s final treasure was also taken and is the only one which cannot be recovered here: the Breastplate of Mytros, taken by Commander Gaius, who delved into the Necropolis months before and overcame the Tomb of Xander and the minotaur himself.

Lastly, his general behavior is still dictated by how the heroes approached the test in the Chamber of Supplicants (area 23). If they have degraded themselves and begged for their right to enter his tomb, he will treat them as lesser: he will not truly believe in their resolve or capabilities, especially compared to Commander Gaius, and will have less patience for conversation (though he is never rude to them), more quickly stepping back into his sarcophagus in silence. If they have looked his depiction in the eye and entered with firmness of purpose, it will be the opposite: he will see them as potential equals, in resolve if not in might - yet, and will be more willing to offer some conversion (though, even willingly, he cannot speak for long).

 Xander’s former armor, the Breastplate of Mytros, has been taken by Commander Gaius - who stoically withstood the effects of the curse, taking the item as part of his legacy - and cannot be found here.
